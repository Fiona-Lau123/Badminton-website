BADMINTON RULES

GENERAL RULE

To win the game, one had to get 21 points to win the entire game but there should be a difference of 2 points. For example, if both are on 20 points, one person will have to get 22 points to win (2-point difference). If the shuttlecock falls on the line, it’s still in; the line depends on whether one is playing singles or doubles. If the point is on an even number, then the game will start on the right side of the court and if the score is a odd number, then the game will start on the left. When hitting the shuttlecock back, one is only allowed one hit back and if the net is touched with body or racket then the point is given to the opponent.

SINGLE GAMES

For serving: When serving in singles, the bit that is coloured light green is where one can serve to (on opponent side), and if the shuttlecock falls in the light green bit on the opponent side when serving, then there would be a point awarded.

After serving: Everything will be in but the blue coloured part will be out. If the shuttlecock falls on the blue this means it’s out and there would be no point awarded.

DOUBLE GAMES

For serving: serving in double, the bit that is coloured light green is where one can serve to (on opponent side), and if the shuttlecock falls in the light green bit on the opponent side when serving, then there would be a point awarded.

After serving: Everything will be in, which means if the shuttlecock falls in the court, the opponent will get the point.
